The Problem

Healthcare's biggest risks happen between checkups

Most health emergencies don't happen in a hospital — they happen at home, alone, unnoticed, until it's too late.

Missed Medications

Patients managing chronic conditions forget doses, take them late, or double up — with no one tracking adherence in real time.

50%of patients don't take medication as prescribed

Delayed Emergency Response

Falls, cardiac events, and oxygen drops often go undetected until a routine check — losing the minutes that matter most.

9min average delay before help is called

Elderly Patients Living Alone

A growing share of elderly patients live without a caregiver nearby, leaving long unmonitored gaps in their day.

1 in 3seniors live alone, with no live monitoring

Lack of Real-Time Monitoring

Vitals are usually checked once per visit — not continuously — so early warning signs of decline are easy to miss.

0continuous vitals tracked between visits, today

    Emergency Response Center

    When seconds matter, PulseCare doesn't wait

    Every detection method feeds the same response pipeline — fast, automatic, and never silent.

    Fall Detection

    Motion sensors recognize a fall pattern and raise an alert within seconds.

    Low Oxygen Alerts

    A drop in SpO₂ below a safe threshold triggers an immediate notification.

    SOS Activation

    One button press starts the full emergency notification sequence instantly.

    Family Notifications

    Designated family members are alerted with the patient's status and location.

    Ambulance Alerts

    For critical events, emergency services can be notified with vitals attached.

    Emergency Workflow

    T+0s

    Abnormal vital or fall pattern detected by the wristband

    T+2s

    Event streamed to the cloud and cross-checked against thresholds

    T+4s

    PulseCare platform raises an alert and opens the patient's live status

    T+6s

    Family and doctors notified simultaneously with current vitals

    T+10s

    Ambulance alerted automatically if the event is classified critical
